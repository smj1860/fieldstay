# Vendor Payment Setup — Stripe Connect

For vendors to receive payment through FieldStay for completed work orders, they need to connect their bank account through Stripe Connect. This is a one-time setup that takes about three to five minutes. Once set up, payments go directly from your account to theirs — no checks, no manual transfers, no follow-up.

---

## Why Vendors Need to Set This Up

FieldStay processes vendor payments through Stripe, the same payment platform used by millions of businesses worldwide. Stripe requires vendors to complete a short identity and banking verification before they can receive funds. This is a compliance requirement — it cannot be skipped.

Until a vendor completes Stripe setup, they can still receive and sign off on work orders, but they cannot receive electronic payment through FieldStay. You would need to pay them outside the platform.

---

## What the Vendor Receives

When you assign a vendor to their first work order, FieldStay automatically sends them a **payment setup email** alongside the work order dispatch. The email explains:

- That you use FieldStay for property operations
- That to receive payment for completed work, they need to connect their bank account
- How long it takes (three to five minutes)
- What they'll need (bank account and routing numbers)

The email contains a secure button — **Set Up My Payout Account** — that takes them directly into the Stripe onboarding flow.

The setup email is separate from the work order dispatch email. Vendors receive both.

---

## What Vendors Need to Complete Setup

- Legal name (or business name if they operate as a company)
- Date of birth (for identity verification — required by Stripe for compliance)
- Bank account and routing number for where payments will be deposited
- Social Security Number or EIN (last 4 digits for most vendors; full number for higher-volume accounts — Stripe determines this based on payout volume)

This is standard information that any payment processor requires. FieldStay and Stripe handle it securely and in compliance with financial regulations.

---

## Where Vendors Do the Setup

Vendors click the link in their setup email and complete the process on Stripe's secure platform. They are never asked to enter banking information directly in a FieldStay page. If a vendor is concerned about security, you can reassure them that Stripe is used by companies like Amazon, Lyft, and Shopify for contractor payments.

---

## How to Tell If a Vendor Has Completed Setup

In your FieldStay dashboard, go to **Settings → Vendors** and open the vendor's profile. The payment status will show one of:

- **Connected** — setup is complete, they can receive electronic payment
- **Pending** — they started but haven't finished the Stripe flow
- **Not Connected** — they haven't started setup yet

---

## Resending the Setup Email

If a vendor lost the original setup email or never received it:

1. Go to **Settings → Vendors**
2. Open the vendor's profile
3. Click **Resend Payment Setup Email**

This sends a fresh setup link to their email address on file.

---

## What Happens If a Vendor Never Completes Setup

If a vendor doesn't complete Stripe setup before a work order is approved and ready for payment, FieldStay will hold the payment in your account. The invoice will show as **Payment Pending — Vendor Setup Required**.

You have two options:
- Wait for the vendor to complete setup, then release payment
- Pay the vendor outside FieldStay and mark the invoice as manually settled

Vendors who consistently don't complete setup should have their payment setup email resent or be reminded directly — the setup is straightforward and only needs to be done once.

---

## Vendor Setup and the Work Order Timeline

The setup email goes out when a vendor is first assigned a work order. We send it early — before the work is done — so that by the time you're ready to release payment, the vendor is already set up and payment clears immediately.

This is intentional. The worst time to discover a vendor isn't set up for payment is after you've approved their work and are ready to pay them.
